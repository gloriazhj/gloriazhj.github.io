Hi!

I am an experimental physicist working in atomic, molecular, and optical (AMO)
physics and quantum information science. I am currently an IQUIST Postdoctoral
Fellow at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where I work on
neutral-atom tweezer arrays using <sup>171</sup>Yb. I completed my PhD at Duke
University, working on trapped-ion quantum computing.

My work centers on building high-fidelity, scalable quantum hardware: designing
and calibrating entangling gates, simulating open-system dynamics, and connecting
device-level physics to the requirements of fault-tolerant quantum computing. I am
comfortable both at the optics table and in the control and simulation stack.

You can read more about my current projects on the [research](/research/) page,
or see my [CV](/cv/) for a full summary of my background and publications.

On the classical side of my life, I enjoy reading, long-distance running and 
spending time with my cats.
