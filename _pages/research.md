My research develops the physics and engineering needed to operate quantum
hardware at high fidelity and scale, across both neutral atom and trapped-ion
platforms. A few current and recent threads:

## Neutral-atom tweezer arrays with <sup>171</sup>Yb

I help buiding and maintaining an up to 40-site <sup>171</sup>Yb atom array, 
performing and benchmarking coherent/incoherent operations over the optical-metastable
-ground (OMG) energylevels. I also participated in generating and characterizing direct
entanglement between atomic qubits and telecom-band photons.

## High-fidelity Rydberg gates and quantum optimal control

I design two-qubit Rydberg-based entangling gates for neutral atom qubits and develop optimal-control
methods (e.g. GRAPE) to make them suitable for our certain experimental parameters,
for instance, differential Doppler shifts on Rydberg transitions seen by two atoms.

## Quantum error correction at the circuit level

I am interested in thinking about how near-term fault-tolerant quantum computing architecture
will look like. Specifically, on neutral atom array platforms, how can we use the reconfigurable
architecture and transversal gate gadget to scale up the system and maintaining a high clock speed?
I have done numerical simulations on small code performances under certain hardware performances, and
I look forward to new opportunities towards realizing near-term FTQEC architecture.

## Trapped-ion quantum computing (PhD, Duke)

My trapped-ion experience in my Ph. D includes designing and characterizing a cryogenic trapped-ion
platform, designing robust two-qubit gate pulses against trap frequency drifts, and quantum simulation
using spin-boson interactions between trapped-ion qubits and collective motional modes in a long ion
chain.
